Within the Daoist community, the body is considered as a microcosm of the universe, the top corresponding to heaven, lightness, insubstantiality. The perineum is considered the sea bottom, the very deepest valley, the most dense, coarse, material aspects of reality. These two are mutually arising and support one another. The coarse, material aspects, create a structure which allows the rarefied energy to circulate. Matter and energy interchange and can be considered as poles of a spectrum.

Meditative practices build on this principle, negative thoughts are ascribed a certain density, they weigh us down, reinforce limitations, lower the vitality of the spirit. Therefore these dense thoughts are sunk to the earth, like current flowing into a vast expanse to be neutralized. Whereas the positive, uplifting, creative, vitalizing, imaginative capacity is cultivated, rarefied, heightened, and developed.

 

Plants too, have a living essence. Plants and people are also interrelated phenomenon, like matter and energy. When we experience the essence of other living creatures we are able to contemplate 'the aesthetic' of life. As an artisan, I have personally curated this particular combination of naturally occurring forest terpenoids in order to communicate a larger idea. I urge you to contemplate this fragrance, revisit it, time and time again, begin a didactic process... as the properties of these terpenoids are truly transformative. In developing this practice you will continually ponder the aesthetic of another naturally occurring phenomena of life. Somaticly experience the essence of plant matter. In contemplating the essence (Jing) we raise the spirit (Shen), invigorate the life force (Qi). These are the three jewels of Taoism. Enjoy.
