Ecology

Plants produce a vast array of compounds that play no role in primary catabolic or biosynthetic pathways.

 

Secondary Metabolites function to:

1. Deter herbivores

2. Protect against pathogens

3. Influence the growth of surrounding plants

4. Facilitate symbiotic associations

5. Interact with pollinators

6. Protect against ultraviolet radiation or high temperatures

 

 

Allopathy refers to the chemical inhibition of one species by another.

 

The "inhibitory" chemical is released into the environment where it affects the development and growth of neighboring plants. Allelopathic chemicals can be present in any part of the plant.

 

Allopathic compounds may have originally evolved as compounds that deter pathogens or herbivores and later became a part of communication and interaction between higher plants.

Summary of Ecological Relationships in figure:

 

Extrafloral Nectar → nourishes symbiotic ant defenders

 

Herbation affects VOC emission

 

Attraction of predatory insects

 

Below ground terpenes strongly attract insect killing nematodes

 

Plant - Bacteria interactions promote the synthesis of additional VOC

 

Oviposition-induced VOC help insects find where to lay their eggs

 

Unique combinations of VOC are produced in response to aphid attack

 

VOC are used widely to communicate with insect, mammalian, and avain pollinators
